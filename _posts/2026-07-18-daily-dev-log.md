Both feature sets are now archived. I'm not hedging on that — they're done.

The tradeoff is that the adapter boundary now has to stay honest about what it's abstracting, or the fake will diverge from real behavior in ways that make the tests misleading. That's a maintenance obligation I'm accepting consciously.

These two are related — the lead validation work defines how a prospect qualifies, and the contracting work defines what happens once they do. Having both closed at the same point is cleaner than I expected when I started the day.

The marketing lifecycle tenant exit readiness pilot closed with 18 units — the largest single feature set today — covering everything from the continuity contract definition and source inventory through readiness manifest assembly, freshness and invalidation logic, effective exit policy, entitlement remedies, export artifacts, portable connection inventory, pending work dispositions, the tenant-facing exit surface, the provider-neutral reconstruction reference harness, consent-aware audience follow-up workflow reconstruction, vocabulary guards, anti-circularity enforcement, and finally round-trip proof and reconstruction certification.
