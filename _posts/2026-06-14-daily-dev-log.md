## Today's Update

Today was about completing two massive architectural audits that I've been building toward for weeks - a comprehensive compliance audit covering everything from CCPA readiness to AI governance, and the full implementation of both the Intelligence Control Plane and Evaluation Registry systems. What started as routine compliance work turned into a complete foundational overhaul of how the platform will handle regulatory requirements and automated decision-making.

The compliance audit was genuinely exhausting - 47 separate work units covering every regulatory surface I could think of. I started with the official 2026 legal citation baseline, then worked through tenant classification readiness, CCPA/CPRA consumer rights, and the Delete Act orchestration. The nastiest part was auditing AI memory and vector embedding supply-chain semantics - tracing how training data lineage flows through the ML pipeline and ensuring we can actually delete derived representations when required. The marketing email and FTC claims risk audit surfaced some issues I hadn't considered around consent management that'll need attention before any real tenant onboarding. By the time I finished documenting architectural gaps and creating the readiness matrix, I had a 95-item inventory of exactly where the platform stands legally.

Parallel to that, I built out the Intelligence Control Plane from scratch - migrations, models, Actions, API routes, the works. The ICP handles ingestion runs, evidence linking, and review workflows for market intelligence data. The tenant scope investigation was particularly interesting because it forced me to justify why MI data should be an exception to the standard tenant isolation model. I ended up documenting it as a justified exception rather than bending the architecture, which feels like the right call. The evidence provenance tests were crucial since this system will need to maintain audit trails for any automated decisions made from the intelligence data.

The Evaluation Registry implementation was the most complex piece - 36 work units covering everything from domain enums to weighted rule evaluation. This system lets tenants define their own compliance standards and evaluation criteria, then automatically score outcomes against those standards. The deterministic rule evaluator was straightforward, but the weighted scoring system required building essentially a mini query engine that can aggregate scores across multiple criteria types. The lifecycle and versioning tests were critical since evaluation standards need to be append-only for audit purposes - you can't retroactively change how something was evaluated six months ago.

This work establishes the regulatory and intelligence foundation the platform needs before any serious tenant onboarding. The compliance audit gives me a concrete remediation roadmap, while the ICP and ER systems provide the infrastructure for automated compliance monitoring and evidence management that'll be essential at scale.
