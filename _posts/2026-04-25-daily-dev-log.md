## Today's Update

Today was the day I finally tackled the horizontal slice backlog that's been haunting my peripheral vision for weeks. I completed 182 tasks across 12 different feature areas, but the real story is that I systematically closed out 8 complete horizontal slices - each one representing weeks of accumulated technical debt that was starting to impact velocity across the platform.

The most significant work went into two major architectural initiatives: the ATM (Autonomous Trust Management) enhancements and the PolicyGovernance compliance documentation system. For ATM, I built out the entire trust fabric from the ground up - extended the TrustTier enum to support a 5-tier model with INTERNAL and UNKNOWN classifications, created the TrustFabric container scaffold, and implemented the complete policy decision pipeline with ActionProposal DTOs and PolicyDecision recording. The PolicyEngine service integration with ToolExecutionPolicy preflight checks required some careful thinking about execution boundaries. I also added the WellKnownManifestController and manifest signing support, which gives external systems a standard way to discover and verify our trust policies.

The PolicyGovernance container was equally substantial - I scaffolded the entire compliance documentation system with migrations for jurisdictions, policy_types, clauses, and policy_versions tables, then built out the policy compilation pipeline with clause selection, trigger evaluation, and content rendering. The PolicyCompilerService turned out to be more complex than expected because it needs to handle client-specific variable injection and regulatory profile matching. I got the complete publish/approval workflow implemented, including the audit trail and provenance tracking that compliance teams will need for regulatory reporting.

What struck me about the horizontal slice work was how interconnected everything became once I started pulling threads. The UI foundation inline-style tokenization work revealed dependencies in the FormRequest adoption patterns, which connected to the observability correlation propagation issues I'd been seeing in the queue retry logic. I ended up refactoring transport boundaries across frontend fetch paths, hardening controller validation patterns, and fixing correlation header propagation - all because the WooCommerce and Shopify sync dashboards needed proper styling tokens. The test stability work was similarly interconnected; unskipping the DebounceManager and FileSystemWatcher test suites required fixing underlying timing issues that were also affecting the accessibility test coverage.

The git history shows a lot of untracked dependency churn alongside the planned work - probably inevitable when you're touching this many moving parts simultaneously. Tomorrow I'll start building business logic on top of these newly solid foundations, beginning with the policy decision UI components and the manifest verification service integration.
