## Today's Update

I worked through a massive architectural audit today - 17 different worksheets covering everything from compliance clocks to quality ratchets. This was the deep review I'd been putting off because it felt abstract, but it turned out to be exactly what the platform needed before moving toward any kind of production readiness.

The compliance and safety work formed the backbone of today's effort. I defined compliance clocks for legal determinations, mapped out operational safety nets, and established quality ratchets that will prevent future architectural drift. The tenancy model bypass closure work was particularly important - I had to trace through every possible path where a request could skip tenant isolation checks. The audit breadth for observers and command gates forced me to think systematically about what gets logged and what gets gated at each layer of the stack.

Parallel to the audit work, I completed a major admin container delegation push - 35 different handoffs moving functionality from the admin container to proper domain containers. This included delegating flow listings to core forms, moving account context tenancy lookups out of admin, and properly routing integration health checks to their appropriate containers. The work was methodical but necessary - the admin container had become a dumping ground for cross-cutting concerns that belonged elsewhere in the Porto architecture.

I also shipped the planning index regeneration system, which includes a Python generator script, metadata schema documentation, and integration with the pre-commit hooks. The backfill work across completed plans was tedious but crucial - without proper metadata, the planning workflow becomes tribal knowledge instead of something transferable. The theme model snapshot runtime gate got fully implemented too, closing a potential security gap in the multi-tenant product surface eligibility checks.

The architectural review audit gives me clear boundaries for what needs hardening before any production deployment. The admin delegation work means the container boundaries are much cleaner, which should make future feature development more predictable.
